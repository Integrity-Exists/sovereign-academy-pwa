What to Do If DSS Shows Up

  1. Stay calm and do not allow entry without a warrant.
  2. Ask for identification and request their business card.
  3. Do not answer questions without legal representation.
  4. Document everything: who came, what time, what was said.
  5. Politely refuse entry unless they present a valid court order or there’s a clear emergency.

⚖️ Know Your Rights

You don’t need a law degree to know how to protect yourself. Start here:

  • 📜 You have the right to remain silent.
    You do NOT have to answer questions from DSS or sign anything without a lawyer.
  • 🏠 You have the right to privacy.
    DSS cannot enter your home without a court order or clear emergency.
  • 󰥕 You have the right to legal representation.
    If you’re being investigated or brought to court, you can ask for a public defender or legal aid.
  • 📸 You have the right to record.
    In Virginia, you can legally record conversations you are a part of — protect yourself by documenting.
  • 🛑 You can say NO.
    You are allowed to refuse drug tests, interviews, or home inspections unless ordered by a judge.

When in doubt, ask Sage. It’s better to be informed than intimidated.

What to Do If You're Arrested

Being arrested can be a frightening experience, but knowing your rights and how to respond can make a significant difference. Here's what you should do:

1. Remain Calm and Composed

Stay calm and do not resist arrest, even if you believe it's unjust. Resisting can lead to additional charges and potential harm.

2. Exercise Your Right to Remain Silent

You have the right to remain silent. Clearly state, "I am exercising my right to remain silent." Do not provide explanations or excuses.

3. Request an Attorney Immediately

Ask for a lawyer as soon as possible. Say, "I want to speak to a lawyer." The police must stop questioning you until your attorney is present.

4. Do Not Consent to Searches

Politely refuse any searches of your person, vehicle, or property. Say, "I do not consent to a search."

5. Know Your Rights

  • You have the right to a local phone call. If you call a lawyer, the police cannot listen.
  • You have the right to know the charges against you.
  • You have the right to a fair trial.

6. Document the Encounter

As soon as possible, write down everything you remember, including officers' badge numbers, patrol car numbers, and any other relevant details.

7. Do Not Discuss Your Case

Avoid discussing your case with anyone other than your attorney. This includes phone calls from jail, as they may be recorded.

8. Follow Legal Advice

Trust your attorney's guidance throughout the legal process. They are there to protect your rights and advocate on your behalf.
